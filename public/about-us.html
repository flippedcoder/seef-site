About Us

The Simon Estes Educational Foundation, Inc. is a multi-cultural non-profit Oklahoma organization that is committed to promoting higher educational opportunities for graduating Tulsa-area high school students.

Together, we can help the next generation get the education they need.

Since the Simon Estes Educational Foundation, Inc was founded in 1983, we have been chasing the ambitious goal: helping proven, high-achieving students reach their goals. We have help students from all backgrounds and mentored them through their undergraduate years, ensuring that we do everything possible for them to graduate. If we work together, we believe any Tulsa area student should be able to afford higher education to reach their dreams.

Our Story

The Simon Estes Educational Foundation, Inc. was founded in 1983. Led by Jayne L. Reed, a small group of Tulsans met with opera star Simon Estes, in town to perform with the Tulsa Opera. Bound by a mutual belief in the importance of education and concern for the many bright Tulsa-area high school seniors financially unable to attend college, the group decided to do what they could to assist these promising students. Mr. Estes and Ms. Reed serve as Co-Founders of the Foundation. Further showing his support, Mr. Estes has made numerous gratuitous concert appearances in Tulsa to benefit the Foundation.

For over thirty years, the Foundation has raised several million dollars towards scholarships to assist hundreds of qualified, graduating Tulsa-area high school seniors. These Simon Estes Scholars have attended some of this nation's most prestigious colleges and universities with some participating in international study programs abroad.

This list includes: Baylor, Cornell, Eastman School of Music, Harvard, Howard, Loyola, The Massachusetts Institute of Technology, Morehouse, Northeastern State University, Notre Dame, Oklahoma State University, Rice, Spelman, St. Louis University, University of Denver, University of Michigan, University of Missouri at Kansas City, The University of Oklahoma, University of Southern California, University of Texas at Austin, The University of Tulsa, Vanderbilt, Washington University at St. Louis, William Jewell, and Yale.

Academic pursuits range from Accounting, Aerospace, Computer Science, Education, Engineering, International Business, Journalism, to Pharmacy, Physics, Pre-Law, Pre-Medicine, Pre-Veterinary Medicine, Sociology, Speech Pathology, and Water Resource Management. All graduates have taken their places in the professional world and serve as the greatest endorsement of the Foundation's work.

The Founders

Jayne Reed

It is my privilege to serve as Co-Founder of The Simon Estes Educational Foundation, Inc. and the extraordinarily talented Simon Estes Scholars who allow me to participate in the development of their futures through education.

In 1982, I was inspired to establish this organization after consistently witnessing the myriad of college- age Tulsa youth working at jobs in the service sector for minimum wages. When questioned about their aspirations they would invariably muse about going to college “some day when I have enough money.” Further conversation revealed their histories of academic excellence, often interspersed by atypical family dynamics. Believing that education is the single most consistent and powerful means for advancement of an individual – I knew that I had to help young people like these.

It is through the generosity of local business leaders and individuals, who commit their time, talent, and resources that The Simon Estes Educational Foundation, Inc. continues to fulfill its founding purpose of providing college scholarships to well-deserving Tulsa-area students.

Simon Estes

Known throughout the world for his opera, concert, and recital performances as well as his recordings, Simon Estes still finds time to share his love and knowledge of music with students from elementary school through college. In his master classes he teaches not only music, but artistic and personal values as well.

Since 1985, Mr. Estes has taught at the Juilliard School of Music. His love and concern for youth is also manifested in the four scholarship organizations he has created. The Simon Estes Educational Foundation, Inc. in Tulsa is the most broad-based. Annually, a selection of students are offered major scholarships to enable them to attend the universities of their choice and pursue chosen majors.

The success of this Foundation has spawned the formation of The Simon Estes International Foundation, Inc., Zurich, Switzerland in 1984 and The Simon Estes Foundation, Cape Town, South Africa in 1996. Restricted music scholarships are offered in Estes’ name at Centerville Community College, the University of Iowa, and through the Simon Estes Iowa Arts Scholarship Fund.

The Board

Glenna Huber

The Rev. Glenna Huber is the 15th Rector of the Church of the Epiphany in Washington, DC. Beforejoining Epiphany, she served as Vicar at the Holy Nativity Episcopal Church in Baltimore, Md. She serves on several boards including The Bishop Claggett Camp and Conference Center, Grace Episcopal Day School, and numerous others.

Suzanne Lee

Suzanne Lee is a Tulsa-area educator who has worked tirelessly to bring a better education to students. She holds a Masters in History and a Masters in Education from the University of Tulsa and an Ed.D from Oklahoma State University. In 2000, she joined three other educators to found a charter school--Tulsa School of Arts and Sciences (TSAS). After two years as the first Director of TSAS, she returned to the classroom.
